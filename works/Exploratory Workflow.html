Exploratory Workflow

An Odyssey from Coral to Digital Confluence

Published on Artnet News

"In every grain of sand there is a story of the earth." If this is true for sand, what tales might a coral whisper to us if we truly listened? In the age where technology threads seamlessly with art, the "Exploratory Workflow" project seeks to unravel these whispered tales and shape them through layers of both the digital and the physical.

As we oscillate between the tactile and the virtual in this "Exploratory Workflow," we touch upon a profound philosophical musing: In our age, what truly is the distinction between the tangible and intangible? The 'real' and the 'imagined'? This project is not just about the coral, art, or technology. It's an exploration of existence in a world where boundaries are constantly redefined.
Amidst this transformation from vacuum forming, a tale from the annals of humanity emerges. As the coral takes shape, it is sheathed in plastic, drawing parallels to a time when humans, in their ceaseless endeavor to preserve, enveloped live corals in a protective shield of plastic. These actions, though undertaken with the hope of conserving these fragile marine entities from external threats, were ironically emblematic of our sometimes misguided interventions with nature.
The plastic-wrapped coral, then, isn't merely a physical form; it's a poignant narrative of humanity's dance with nature, an oscillation between protection and domination, care and oblivion. As observers witness this stage, they are gently nudged to reflect on our place in the natural world and the consequences, both intended and unforeseen, of our touch upon it.
By the journey's end, participants are left with more than just an experience. They're left with a contemplation – on art, nature, technology, and the intriguing intersections of these realms. In the digital age, where everything can be replicated, reimagined, and reborn, what becomes of the essence of the original? And more importantly, as creators and observers, where do we stand amidst these shifting sands?
